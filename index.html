Our Mission

Where Ancient Wisdom
Meets Modern Science

Mindfulness on the Mats™ bridges 2,500 years of mindful martial arts tradition with cutting-edge neuroscience — giving martial arts schools a ready-to-use curriculum that makes their students sharper, calmer, and more powerful both on and off the mats.

The ancient masters called it Zanshin — total awareness of yourself and your surroundings. We call it the Mindful Warrior. And now, for the first time, every school can teach it.

Founded by Dr. Joseph Assouline, PsyD

Clinical psychologist with over twenty years of experience practicing and teaching the martial arts. Dr. Assouline combines his clinical expertise with deep martial arts knowledge to create a curriculum that is both scientifically grounded and practically applicable.

The Science

What Happens to the
Brain on the Mats

Mindfulness doesn't just change how students feel — it physically restructures the brain. Decades of peer-reviewed neuroscience back up what martial arts masters have known for centuries.

🧠
The Amygdala — Panic Button

Mindfulness practice actually shrinks the amygdala, the brain's alarm bell. Students react less to fear, intimidation, and high-pressure moments.

⚡
The Prefrontal Cortex — The Commander

Cortical thickening in the decision-making region. Students make better calls under pressure and gain greater impulse control.

📚
The Hippocampus — The Librarian

Increased gray matter density. Students learn techniques faster, retain more, and manage training stress with greater ease.

Performance Benefits

The Edge Every
Competitor Wants

01
Faster Reaction Time

A fraction of a second often determines the winner. Mindfulness training improves working memory and sustains attention under combat conditions.

02
Flow State Accessibility

The state of Mushin — where you move without hesitation — is not luck. It is a trainable skill. MOTM gives students direct access to it.

03
Pain Management & Endurance

Research shows mindfulness alters the subjective experience of pain, enabling higher thresholds during intense training and competition.

04
Emotional Regulation

The ability to pause between trigger and response — the defining skill of the disciplined martial artist, now trainable in every class.
